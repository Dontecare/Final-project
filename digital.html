Digital Art Process

The genesis of each piece is rooted in a profound realization of my journey navigating the world as an individual on the autism spectrum. The inspiration stems from the persistent struggle to be understood beyond the surface, as people often form judgments based solely on appearances. Recognizing the unique lens through which I perceive the world, I embark on a creative journey to unravel the complexities that lie beneath the initial impressions.

To translate this intricate inspiration into a concrete concept, I employ a method that mirrors the nuances of my perception. Building upon commonly recognized visuals, I deconstruct the world into shapes and patterns. Each element becomes a building block, allowing me to convey the intricacies of my visual experience, shape by shape, pattern by pattern.

The execution phase unfolds digitally, with Adobe Illustrator as my canvas for drawing and Adobe Photoshop as the medium for arranging the pieces. The digital realm provides the perfect platform to manifest my conceptual vision. Through the meticulous arrangement of abstract shapes, I craft a visual narrative that captures the essence of my unique perspective.

As the pieces come together, I engage in a critical reflection on the work. I contemplate what may be missing or how my intended message could be better conveyed. This reflective process serves as a refinement stage, where I fine-tune elements to ensure the artwork aligns seamlessly with my vision and effectively communicates the intricate layers of my perspective.

The journey concludes when the piece not only harmonizes into a coherent drawing or rendering but also adheres to the fundamental rule governing my creative processâ€”using and layering abstract shapes to construct a bigger picture. The completion brings a sense of fulfillment, as the artwork stands as a testament to the unique way I navigate and interpret the world, inviting others to perceive its depths with fresh eyes.
